Classification Tree Results

Explore the predictors of college student placement and make predictions on whether someone will be accepted based on various independent variables.

This project examined various predictors of college student placement.

The root node (the starting point) is the communication score, which splits at 7.5

The other decision nodes include IQ (110.5), CGPA (8.005), and projects (1.5)

For example, the results show us that scoring less than 7.5 on communication and having an IQ of less than 110.5 does not result in a placement (52.92% of the sample).

For another example, students who received a communication score of less than 7.5, had an IQ of greater than 110.5, a cumulative GPA of greater than 8.005, and greater than 1.5 projects do receive a placement (4.33%)

The analysis (classification trees) was done in R, and the PDF from R is available to show the code used.
